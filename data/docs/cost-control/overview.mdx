## Overview

Telemetry data has a cost. In SigNoz Cloud it is a direct billing cost based on ingestion volume.

<Admonition type="note">
**Self-Hosted:** In Self-Hosted deployments, cost manifests as ClickHouse storage, query performance, and OTel Collector resource consumption.
</Admonition>

In both cases, a large fraction of ingested volume comes from a small number of high-volume sources — high-cardinality metrics, health check routes generating millions of spans, or verbose debug logs.

This guide walks you through identifying and reducing that volume across three phases: **Analysis, Execution, and Validation**.

## Optimization Flow

### Phase 1: Analysis

Identify which signal, service, and environment is driving your ingestion volume. Use the right SigNoz feature for the time window you are investigating.

**[Analyze Ingestion Volume](https://signoz.io/docs/cost-control/understand-ingestion-volume/)**

### Phase 2: Execution

Once you have identified the high-volume source, apply the appropriate fix.

<Admonition type="tip">
**Source First, Collector Second:** Fixing the problem at source — in your application code or SDK configuration — reduces load across the entire pipeline and applies to all downstream consumers of that telemetry. Use Collector-level filtering when you do not have access to the source, or when multiple services share the same high-volume pattern and a single Collector rule is more practical than changing each service individually.
</Admonition>

Proceed to the guide for the signal you want to reduce:

- **[Optimize Metrics](https://signoz.io/docs/cost-control/optimize-metrics/):** Drop unused metrics or aggregate high-cardinality labels.
- **[Optimize Traces](https://signoz.io/docs/cost-control/optimize-traces/):** Reduce trace volume through head-based or tail-based sampling.
- **[Optimize Logs](https://signoz.io/docs/cost-control/optimize-logs/):** Filter verbose logs at the Collector.

### Phase 3: Validation

After deploying a change, verify the result.

1. The Cost Meter Dashboard uses `signoz.meter.*` metrics which aggregate hourly. For immediate feedback, use the relevant Explorer (Logs, Traces, or Metrics Explorer) to confirm the change is taking effect.
2. Configure **[Cost Meter Alerts](https://signoz.io/docs/cost-meter/alerts/)** to be notified when ingestion volume crosses your expected threshold.

### Non-Production Enforcements

To apply hard ingestion caps on Dev or Staging environments regardless of telemetry content *(SigNoz Cloud only)*:

**[Ingestion Limits](https://signoz.io/docs/cost-control/ingestion-limits/)**
