# Optimize Logs

This guide covers how to reduce log ingestion volume in SigNoz by filtering low-value log records before they reach ingestion.

Log ingestion volume is measured in **Gigabytes (GB)** and is often dominated by verbose `DEBUG` output and repetitive infrastructure probes.

If your application's logging framework supports level configuration, setting the appropriate log level at source is more effective than Collector-level filtering — it prevents the log from being generated at all. If you do not have control over the source, use the Collector-level approach below.

## When Logs Are Your Focus

- The **Cost Meter Dashboard** shows log GB (`signoz.meter.log.size`) as your primary cost driver, or log record count (`signoz.meter.log.count`) is notably high.
- **Ingestion Analysis** shows `DEBUG` severity dominance across your highest-volume service.

## Filter Logs at the Edge

Use the `filter` processor before ingestion. Filtering at the OpenTelemetry Collector ensures data is dropped before it reaches SigNoz.

**Common Filter Rules:**

```yaml
processors:
  filter/logs:
    error_mode: ignore
    log_record_conditions:
      # Drop DEBUG logs except from services where DEBUG is operationally required
      - 'severity_text == "DEBUG" and attributes["service.name"] != "payment-service"'

      # Drop Kubernetes liveness and readiness probe logs
      - 'IsMatch(attributes["k8s.pod.name"], "liveness|readiness")'

      # Drop DEBUG output from a specific instrumentation library
      - 'instrumentation_scope.name == "urllib3" and severity_text == "DEBUG"'
```

## Decision Guide

| Log Signature | Recommendation |
| --- | --- |
| `DEBUG` from infrastructure or third-party libraries | Generally safe to drop |
| `DEBUG` from your own services | Review before dropping — may contain useful diagnostic context |
| `ERROR` / `WARN` | Retain |
| `INFO` from business transaction logic | Review before dropping — high operational value |
| Any severity from Kubernetes health check probes | Generally safe to drop |

## Optimisation Trade-Offs

| Approach | Volume Reduction | Risk |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Filter Processor** (targeted rules) | High | Low — rules are explicit and scoped |
| **Blanket `DEBUG` filter** (drop all `DEBUG` across all services) | Medium | Low — verify no critical service emits only at `DEBUG` |
| **Blanket `INFO` filter** (drop all `INFO` across all services) | High | Medium — risks losing transaction flow context |

👉 **Read more:** [Filtering and Dropping Logs](https://signoz.io/docs/logs-management/guides/drop-logs/)

## Validate Your Changes

<Admonition type="note">
The Cost Meter Dashboard uses `signoz.meter.*` metrics which aggregate hourly — changes may take up to one hour to appear there. Use the Logs Explorer for faster feedback first.
</Admonition>

To confirm the volume reduction:

1. Navigate to **Logs**, swap to the **Table** tab. Filter by your target service and group by `severity_text` — verify the log counts for the severities you targeted have reduced.
2. Wait 1 hour, then check the **Cost Meter Dashboard** filtered by your target service and confirm the log GB trend has dropped.
